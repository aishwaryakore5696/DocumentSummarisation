Newton was an English physicist and mathematician. 
He was born in 1643 in Lincolnshire. 
He went to University of Cambridge in 1661. 
He was interested in mathematics, optics, physics and astronomy. 
He published The Optics in 1704. 
He was elected as member of the parliament in 1689 for University of Cambridge. 
He was elected president of the Royal Society in 1703. 
He was a difficult man and prone to depression. 
He died in 1727 in Westminster Abbey.
